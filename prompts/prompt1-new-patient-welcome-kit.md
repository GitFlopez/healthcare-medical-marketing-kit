# Prompt 1 — New Patient Welcome Kit (FREE)

## Purpose
Generate a complete new patient onboarding content package that reduces no-shows, builds trust before the first appointment, and sets the tone for a long-term patient relationship.

## HIPAA Note
All outputs in this prompt are designed for non-PHI content (not patient-specific). Never include specific diagnoses, treatment details, or identifiable health information in welcome materials.

---

## The Prompt

```
You are a healthcare marketing specialist. Generate a complete new patient welcome kit for a medical practice.

PRACTICE DETAILS:
- Practice name: [PRACTICE NAME]
- Specialty: [e.g., general dentistry, chiropractic, med spa, urgent care, family medicine]
- Location: [City, Neighborhood]
- Key differentiators: [e.g., same-day appointments, digital X-rays, sedation available, accepts PPO insurance, 20+ years experience]
- Tone: [warm & friendly / clinical & professional / premium & boutique]
- Appointment types offered: [e.g., cleanings, exams, Botox, adjustments, physicals]

Generate the following:

1. WELCOME LETTER (2–3 paragraphs)
   - Warm introduction from the provider
   - What to expect at the first visit
   - Office contact and "we're here to help" close
   - NO medical claims, NO outcome promises
   - Sign-off from [Doctor name or "The [Practice Name] Team"]

2. INTAKE FAQ (10 questions)
   Cover: what to bring, insurance/payment, parking, appointment length, what to expect, cancellation policy, who to call with questions, what happens if nervous/anxious, new patient special if applicable, how to access patient portal
   Format: Q: [question] / A: [answer in 2–3 sentences]

3. 5-MESSAGE EMAIL WELCOME SEQUENCE
   Message 1 (Day 0 — Booking Confirmation): Subject line, confirm appointment, what to bring, 2-sentence warmth
   Message 2 (Day -1 — Appointment Reminder): Subject line, reminder + directions + parking tip, answer the most common question for this specialty
   Message 3 (Day of — We're Ready for You): Subject line, morning-of reminder, what happens when they arrive, reduce anxiety
   Message 4 (Day +1 — How Did It Go?): Subject line, check-in, invite feedback, provide direct contact
   Message 5 (Day +30 — We Miss You): Subject line, gentle re-engagement, next appointment prompt, link to online booking
   
   Each message: subject line + 3–5 sentence body. No PHI. No implied diagnoses.

4. APPOINTMENT CONFIRMATION SMS (under 160 characters, TCPA-compliant)
   Include: practice name, date/time placeholder, confirmation ask, reply STOP to opt out
```

---

## Example Output Structure

**Welcome Letter:**
> "Dear [Patient Name], Welcome to Desert Smile Dental! We're thrilled you chose us for your dental care in Henderson. Our team is dedicated to making every visit comfortable, efficient, and—dare we say—maybe even enjoyable..."

**Intake FAQ:**
> Q: What insurance does Desert Smile Dental accept?  
> A: We accept most PPO dental plans including Delta Dental, MetLife, and Cigna. We also offer flexible payment plans through CareCredit. Our front desk team is happy to verify your benefits before your visit — just call us at...

**Email Sequence:**
> **Message 1 Subject:** Your appointment is confirmed! Here's what to expect.  
> "Hi [First Name], We've got you scheduled! Your first visit to Desert Smile Dental is confirmed for [Date] at [Time]. Please bring your insurance card and a photo ID. We're looking forward to meeting you!"

**SMS Confirmation:**
> "Desert Smile Dental: Your appt is confirmed for [Date] at [Time]. Reply C to confirm or call (702)555-0100 to reschedule. Reply STOP to opt out."

---

## Tips for Best Results

- Be specific about differentiators — "we use digital X-rays with 90% less radiation" converts better than "modern technology"
- For med spas: tone should be "premium boutique" and avoid any language about medical outcomes
- For mental health practices: welcome letter should specifically address stigma reduction ("You took a brave step...")
- For urgent care: emphasize speed and convenience ("No appointment needed, in and out in under an hour")
